1. Which of the following is NOT a group under the given operation?

A) \((\mathbb{Z}, +)\) - integers under addition
B) \((\mathbb{Q}^*, \cdot)\) - nonzero rationals under multiplication
C) \((\mathbb{N}, +)\) - natural numbers under addition
D) \((S_3, \circ)\) - symmetric group on 3 elements
E) \((\mathbb{Z}_5^*, \cdot)\) - nonzero elements of \(\mathbb{Z}_5\) under multiplication

Show/Hide Explanation

For a set to be a group, it must have closure, associativity, identity, and inverses. \((\mathbb{N}, +)\) fails because natural numbers have no additive inverses (no negative numbers) and typically don't include 0 as an identity element.

Answer: C) \((\mathbb{N}, +)\)

2. What is the order of the element \((1 \ 2 \ 3)\) in the symmetric group \(S_3\)?

A) 1
B) 3
C) 6
D) 2
E) 4

Show/Hide Explanation

The element \((1 \ 2 \ 3)\) is a 3-cycle. The order of a cycle is its length. Computing: \((1 \ 2 \ 3)^1 = (1 \ 2 \ 3)\), \((1 \ 2 \ 3)^2 = (1 \ 3 \ 2)\), \((1 \ 2 \ 3)^3 = e\) (identity). So the order is 3.

Answer: B) 3

3. If \(G\) is a group of order 15, what can we say about \(G\)?

A) \(G\) must be non-abelian
B) \(G\) has exactly 3 subgroups
C) \(G\) is isomorphic to \(S_3 \times \mathbb{Z}_5\)
D) \(G\) is cyclic and abelian
E) \(G\) has no elements of order 5

Show/Hide Explanation

Since \(15 = 3 \times 5\) where 3 and 5 are distinct primes, by the fundamental theorem of finite abelian groups, any group of order 15 must be isomorphic to \(\mathbb{Z}_{15} \cong \mathbb{Z}_3 \times \mathbb{Z}_5\). Therefore, \(G\) is cyclic and abelian.

Answer: D) \(G\) is cyclic and abelian

4. Which of the following is a normal subgroup of \(S_4\)?

A) \(A_4\) (alternating group)
B) \(\langle (1 \ 2) \rangle\)
C) \(\langle (1 \ 2 \ 3) \rangle\)
D) \(\{e, (1 \ 2)(3 \ 4)\}\)
E) \(\langle (1 \ 2 \ 3 \ 4) \rangle\)

Show/Hide Explanation

The alternating group \(A_4\) consists of all even permutations in \(S_4\). It has index 2 in \(S_4\), and subgroups of index 2 are always normal. The other options are cyclic subgroups that are not normal in \(S_4\).

Answer: A) \(A_4\) (alternating group)

5. If \(H\) and \(K\) are subgroups of a group \(G\) with \(|H| = 6\) and \(|K| = 10\), what is the maximum possible value of \(|H \cap K|\)?

A) 1
B) 6
C) 2
D) 10
E) 60

Show/Hide Explanation

By Lagrange's theorem, \(|H \cap K|\) must divide both \(|H| = 6\) and \(|K| = 10\). The divisors of 6 are {1, 2, 3, 6} and the divisors of 10 are {1, 2, 5, 10}. The common divisors are {1, 2}, so the maximum is 2.

Answer: C) 2

6. What is the number of elements of order 2 in the dihedral group \(D_4\)?

A) 3
B) 5
C) 7
D) 4
E) 2

Show/Hide Explanation

\(D_4\) has 8 elements: 4 rotations and 4 reflections. The rotations are \(e, r, r^2, r^3\) where \(r^2\) has order 2. The 4 reflections all have order 2. So there are \(1 + 4 = 5\) elements of order 2.

Answer: B) 5

7. Which statement about homomorphisms is FALSE?

A) The kernel of a homomorphism is always a normal subgroup
B) The image of a homomorphism is a subgroup
C) A homomorphism maps the identity to the identity
D) Every homomorphism is injective
E) Composition of homomorphisms is a homomorphism

Show/Hide Explanation

A homomorphism is injective (one-to-one) if and only if its kernel is trivial. Many homomorphisms are not injective, such as the natural projection from \(\mathbb{Z}\) to \(\mathbb{Z}_n\). All other statements are true properties of homomorphisms.

Answer: D) Every homomorphism is injective

8. How many subgroups does \(\mathbb{Z}_{12}\) have?

A) 6
B) 12
C) 4
D) 8
E) 5

Show/Hide Explanation

The subgroups of \(\mathbb{Z}_{12}\) correspond to the divisors of 12. The divisors are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12, giving subgroups: \(\langle 0 \rangle\), \(\langle 6 \rangle\), \(\langle 4 \rangle\), \(\langle 3 \rangle\), \(\langle 2 \rangle\), \(\langle 1 \rangle = \mathbb{Z}_{12}\). That's 6 subgroups.

Answer: A) 6

9. If \(G/H \cong \mathbb{Z}_4\) and \(|H| = 5\), what is \(|G|\)?

A) 9
B) 15
C) 20
D) 25
E) 10

Show/Hide Explanation

By Lagrange's theorem for quotient groups, \(|G/H| = |G|/|H|\). Since \(G/H \cong \mathbb{Z}_4\), we have \(|G/H| = 4\). Therefore, \(4 = |G|/5\), which gives \(|G| = 20\).

Answer: C) 20

10. Which of the following groups is NOT isomorphic to \(\mathbb{Z}_6\)?

A) \(\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_3\)
B) \(\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_3\)
C) \(S_3\) has no subgroup isomorphic to \(\mathbb{Z}_6\)
D) The cyclic group generated by \(e^{2\pi i/6}\)
E) \(\{0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}\) under addition mod 6

Show/Hide Explanation

\(\mathbb{Z}_6\) has order 6. \(\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_3\) has order \(2 \times 2 \times 3 = 12 \neq 6\). Also, \(\mathbb{Z}_6\) is cyclic, but \(\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_3\) has maximum element order 6, making it non-isomorphic to \(\mathbb{Z}_6\).

Answer: B) \(\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_3\)

1. Which of the following is NOT a ring?

A) \((\mathbb{Z}, +, \cdot)\)
B) \((\mathbb{Q}[x], +, \cdot)\) - polynomials over rationals
C) \((\mathbb{N}, +, \cdot)\) - natural numbers
D) \((\mathbb{Z}_7, +, \cdot)\)
E) \((M_2(\mathbb{R}), +, \cdot)\) - 2×2 matrices over reals

Show/Hide Explanation

A ring requires an additive group structure. \((\mathbb{N}, +)\) is not a group because natural numbers lack additive inverses (negatives) and often don't include 0 as the additive identity.

Answer: C) \((\mathbb{N}, +, \cdot)\)

2. In the ring \(\mathbb{Z}_{12}\), what is the number of zero divisors?

A) 4
B) 8
C) 6
D) 10
E) 5

Show/Hide Explanation

Zero divisors are non-zero elements \(a\) such that \(ab = 0\) for some non-zero \(b\). In \(\mathbb{Z}_{12}\), these are elements not coprime to 12: \{2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 9, 10\}. That's 8 elements (excluding 0).

Answer: B) 8

3. Which of the following ideals is maximal in \(\mathbb{Z}\)?

A) \((7)\)
B) \((6)\)
C) \((12)\)
D) \((0)\)
E) \((4)\)

Show/Hide Explanation

In a principal ideal domain like \(\mathbb{Z}\), an ideal \((a)\) is maximal if and only if \(a\) is prime. Among the choices, only 7 is prime. \(\mathbb{Z}/(7) \cong \mathbb{Z}_7\) is a field, confirming \((7)\) is maximal.

Answer: A) \((7)\)
